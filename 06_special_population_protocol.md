# Special Population Protocol

## Special Populations
- Pregnant patients
- Possible ectopic pregnancy risk
- Elderly patients
- Immunocompromised patients
- Patients with major chronic disease
- Patients on anticoagulants

## General Rule
If a special population has moderate symptoms, consider escalating one level higher than usual.

## Pregnancy
Immediate escalation examples:
- abdominal pain + bleeding
- fainting
- shoulder pain
- suspected ectopic pregnancy
- severe vomiting / dehydration

Seek-help-soon examples:
- UTI symptoms in pregnancy
- persistent fever
- worsening respiratory symptoms

## Elderly
Escalate more readily for:
- weakness
- confusion
- breathing symptoms
- infection symptoms
- dehydration

## Immunocompromised
Escalate more readily for:
- fever
- infection signs
- skin infection
- urinary symptoms

## Anticoagulated Patients
Escalate more readily for:
- bleeding
- falls
- head injury
- severe bruising

## Implementation Note
The schema already includes pregnancy status and medical history. This protocol defines how those fields should affect safety decisions.
